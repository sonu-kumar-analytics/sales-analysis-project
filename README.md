# 📊 Superstore Sales Analysis Project

## 📌 Project Overview

Businesses generate a large amount of sales data every day, but raw data alone does not help in making better decisions. The real value comes from analyzing that data to understand customer behavior, product performance, and profitability.

In this project, I analyzed retail sales data using SQL, Excel, and Power BI to uncover meaningful insights that can support business decision-making. The project focuses on identifying sales trends, profitable products, high-performing regions, and customer purchasing patterns.

---

## 🎯 Problem Statement

A retail company has been collecting sales data from multiple cities, products, and customer segments. However, the management does not have a clear understanding of which products generate the highest profit, which locations perform best, and where business improvements are needed.

The goal of this project is to analyze historical sales data and transform it into actionable business insights that help improve sales performance and profitability.

---

## 🎯 Business Objectives

- Analyze overall sales performance.
- Identify the most profitable products and categories.
- Discover high-performing cities and regions.
- Find products or categories generating losses.
- Understand customer purchasing behavior.
- Provide recommendations to improve business performance.

---

## 📂 Dataset

The dataset contains retail sales transaction records, including customer information, product details, sales, quantity, profit, discount, category, sub-category, region, city, and order date.

---

## ❓ Business Questions

- What are the total Sales, Profit, Quantity Sold, and Total Orders?
- Which product categories generate the highest sales and profit?
- Which sub-categories are the most and least profitable?
- Which cities and regions contribute the highest revenue?
- Which products are generating losses?
- Who are the top customers by sales?
- How do sales and profit change over time?
- How do discounts affect profitability?

## 💡 Key Insights

- A small number of products contribute a significant portion of total revenue.
- Some categories generate high sales but comparatively low profit.
- Certain cities consistently outperform others in both sales and profitability.
- Sales show noticeable seasonal trends throughout the year.
- A few customers contribute a large percentage of total revenue.
- Higher discounts do not always result in higher profits.

---

## ✅ Business Recommendations

- Focus marketing efforts on high-profit products.
- Reduce discounts on low-margin products.
- Expand business in high-performing cities and regions.
- Review pricing strategies for loss-making products.
- Improve inventory planning based on demand trends.
- Develop customer loyalty programs for high-value customers.
